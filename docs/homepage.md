# Knowsun Homepage — Content Plan

> This document defines the content strategy and section-by-section outline for the redesigned homepage.

---

## Page Structure Recommendation: Single Page

**Recommendation: One page, for now.**

Reasons:
- Portfolio and team are still thin (2 stealth projects, team TBD). Spreading sparse content across 2+ pages weakens both.
- The recruiting pitch (AI-first culture, 3-day workweek, pair programming) is also compelling to clients — it signals efficiency and a modern operation. No need to hide it.
- A single focused page performs better for SEO and first impressions for a boutique firm.
- Revisit splitting into 2 pages (client-facing + careers) once there's enough content to fill them (e.g., 3+ case studies, 5+ team bios).

## Suggested Sections (in order)

### 1. Hero
- **Headline:** Bold, punchy — something like: *"AI-First Consulting for the Businesses Big Tech Ignores"*
- **Subline:** 1–2 sentences positioning Knowsun as the boutique AI/ML partner for small and mid-sized businesses in Michigan and the Midwest.
- **CTA:** "Let's Talk" / "Work With Us" → contact form anchor

> *Design note: Keep it minimal. Full-width, dark navy background, white text — consistent with the old brand identity.*

---

### 2. Our Niche (What We Do)
Core message: The AI era is creating a massive shift toward customized solutions. Large consultancies won't bother with small/mid-sized businesses. Knowsun will.

Key points:
- We help small and medium-sized businesses build AI-first products and workflows.
- We're local-focused: Michigan and the Midwest. We prefer working with clients nearby.
- We pass the productivity gains from AI directly to you — expect better value at reasonable rates.

---

### 3. Why Not Just Use AI Yourself?
This section directly addresses the "why hire consultants when AI can code?" objection. Frame it conversationally.

Key arguments:
- **Experience matters.** It takes skill to drive AI toward good outcomes — knowing what to ask, what to reject, and how to make design trade-offs.
- **Accountability.** AI doesn't own the code, support, or maintenance. We do.
- **Focus on your business.** Even if you *could* build it yourself, should you? Let us handle the AI tooling so you can focus on the value you deliver to your own customers.
- **AI still needs human judgment.** Opinionated architecture decisions, trade-offs, product sense — that's still human work.

---

### 4. Our Approach
Four pillars (short, visual — icons or cards):

1. **Human-Centered AI** — Technology built around real human needs. Dr. Zhou's PhD was literally on this.
2. **AI-First Delivery** — ~99% of code written with AI, guided by experienced engineers. Faster delivery, lower cost.
3. **Reasonable Pricing** — We're exploring new consulting models for the AI era. We want pricing to work for you.
4. **Local Partnership** — We prefer Midwest clients and relationships over remote-only engagements.

### 6. Our Culture *(also serves as recruiting hook)*
This section doubles as a careers pitch. Frame it as "how we work" rather than a job listing.

- **3-Day Workweek.** In the AI era, people should work less. We believe AI multiplies output — so we pass that time back to our team.
- **Pair Programming.** Every unit works in pairs: junior + senior, junior + junior, or senior + senior. Better code, faster learning, shared ownership.
- **AI-Native.** We don't fight AI — we build with it. If you want to work at the frontier of how software gets built, this is the place.
